Egusi Recipe

Here's what our finished soup should look like

If you've been seeing this soup online and wondering if it's really as good as they say, well, you're in the right place because I'll show you how to prepare it yourself and you'll finally get to taste it. Here are all the ingredients you'll need:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup blended onions (about 3- 5 and fresh chilies, to taste)
  • 4 cups egusi (melon seeds, ground or milled)
  • 1/2 to 1 cup palm oil
  • 2 teaspoons fresh Une (Iru, locust beans)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ground crayfish (to taste)
  • 7â€“ 8 cups stock
  • Cooked Meat & fish (quantity and variety to personal preference)
  • 2 cups cut pumpkin leaves
  • 2 cups cut pumpkin leaves
  • 2 tablespoon bitter leaf (washed)

Directions

  1. Make the blended/ground egusi into a thick paste using blended onions
  2. In a large pot, heat the palm oil on medium for a minute and then add the Une(locust beans).
  3. Slowly add the stock and set on low heat to simmer.
  4. Scoop teaspoon size balls of the egusi paste mixture into the stock. Be sure to keep ball shape.
  5. Leave to simmer for 20 - 30 minutes so the balls cook through.
  6. Add the meat and fish and other bits which you'd like to use.
  7. Add cut-up pumpkin and waterleaf leaves.
  8. Add the bitter leaf. Leave the lid off while the cooking finishes for another 5-10 minutes.
  9. Stir, check seasoning and adjust accordingly.

    10. Now you can sit back and enjoy your delicious Egusi soup.
