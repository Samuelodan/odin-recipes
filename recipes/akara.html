Akara (beans fritters)

These delicious pieces, also known as beans fritters, are said to be of Nigerian origin, but can be found in several other African countries. Here are all the ingredients you'll need:

Ingredients

  • Lots of beans
  • Peppers
  • Crayfish
  • Salt
  • Stock cubes
  • Diced onions

Directions

  1. Soak for about 30 minutes or till the skin swells.
  2. Peel off the beans with your hands (the hard way) or use a blender and pulse a couple of times to split the beans (the easy way).
  3. Blend the peeled beans with your peppers, crayfish, salt, and stock cubes.
  4. Whisk until the batter becomes airy and fluffy. This will take about a minute to 5 minutes depending on the tool you use. Electric hand whisk takes about a minute to two, regular hand whisk will take about 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Stir in the diced onions.
  6. Preheat the oil and scoop the mixture by spoonfuls into the pan.
  7. Finally, stir till golden brown.

And that's it, you've now made beautiful Akara. It's often eaten with bread so you can try it out. Cheers!
